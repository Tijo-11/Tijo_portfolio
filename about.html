About Me

Hello! I'm Tijo, and welcome to my portfolio. I’m an aspiring web developer currently undergoing rigorous training at Brototype.

I learn from industry experts through various platforms such as YouTube videos, blogs, and websites. My goal is to create dynamic, user-friendly websites that provide engaging digital experiences, helping businesses connect with their audience.

My journey into web development began with a deep interest in coding, and it’s a journey I continue to pursue, constantly learning and growing in the tech field.

My Objective

To contribute meaningfully to society by embracing knowledge, creativity, and empathy. I aim to create impactful digital experiences, promote awareness around mental health, and foster a world driven by science, rationality, and cultural understanding. Through continuous learning and personal growth, I strive to inspire others while staying committed to making a positive difference in the lives I touch.
My immediate goal is to be a web developer and develop applications and websites that deliver value to users while maintaining simplicity and usability. I aim to enhance my skills further and take on larger, more challenging projects in the future.

Background

With an undergraduate background in Physics, Mathematics, and Electronics, I pursued a master's in Physics driven by my curiosity about the underlying secrets of the universe. However, I found myself dissatisfied with the current research landscape. To support myself, I shifted to preparing students for IIT-JEE and NEET entrance exams. As a Physics teacher, I performed well, with students from my first batch securing admissions in prestigious institutions such as IIT Madras, NIT Trichy, and IIIT Odisha.

I differentiated myself as a teacher by focusing not only on academic success but also on the holistic development of my students. I understood their challenges in learning and suggested better methodologies and more efficient ways to approach problems. Despite the joy I found in my students' achievements, I felt dissatisfied with the repetitive nature of my academic career.

At this point, the field of coding intrigued me. Learning that Sir Tim Berners-Lee, the founder of the World Wide Web and the HTML language, was also a physicist, served as a powerful motivation. The principles behind computing, rooted in semiconductor physics, resonated with my scientific background. This year, the Nobel Prize in Physics was awarded for work applying physics principles to Artificial Intelligence, which further strengthened my belief that my foundation in Physics could be applied to the coding world. This connection sparked my decision to venture into coding, and I am now fully committed to giving it my best try.
